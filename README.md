## Question & Hypothesis 
Did Beijing's air quality measurably improve over the 2013-2017 Air Pollution Action Plan, and which pollutant carries the clearest policy signal once seasonal and meteorological variation is accounted for? 
**Hypothesis: **
Coal-linked SO2 declines sharply and steadily, while PM2.5 improvement is real but smaller and partly masked by year-to-year weather.

## Dataset
Beijing Multi-Site Air-Quality Data (includes 12 monitoring stations,hourly, March 2013–Feb 2017, with PM2.5, PM10, SO2, NO2, CO, O3 and meteorology).
https://archive.ics.uci.edu/dataset/501/beijing+multi+site+air+quality+data

## Summary
We will analyze the dataset to examine whether Beijing's 2013 clean air policies are reflected in citywide pollution trends. After cleaning the timestamped station data, we will use Pandas to aggregate hourly observations into diurnal patterns, seasonal climatologies, and deseasonalized monthly trends for PM2.5, SO2, No2, and PM 10. We will then compare pollutant trajectories and test whether PM2.5 changes persist after accounting for wind speed, wind direction, and temperature, using the resulting figures to seperate policy-related improvement from meteorological variation.
